Floating Roots

This interactive installation explores the cultural, ecological, and spiritual histories of Pokkali rice farming in Kerala. It draws on oral history, sensory interaction, and the interwoven layers of land and labor.

Ceremonies of Pokkali

Through sacred rituals, seasonal transitions, and generational memory, Pokkali farming is as much a ceremony as it is a cultivation method.

Mechanism Description

Ecotourism

Pokkali fields are increasingly being positioned within eco-tourism landscapes. But what stories are prioritized? What’s erased?

Mechanism Description

This segment dissects the infrastructural mechanisms behind "eco" branding — from water taxis to floating pathways — and their ecological impacts.

Working with Water

Pokkali farmers don't fight floods — they work with them. This tab explores the adaptive tools and cyclical technologies behind water-based farming.

Mechanism Description

Mechanisms like tidal gates and floating bunds are explained with illustrations of how they allow saltwater-flooded fields to regenerate naturally.
